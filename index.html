Arkham Horror & Eldritch Madness

Reality crumbles, and you are left to piece together the screaming silence.

Inspired by Arkham Horror and cosmic dread. A world where mystery, madness, and myth intertwine. Clues don’t just reveal answers—they reveal the truth you wish you didn’t know.

💀 What Defines This World Type?

  • Sanity systems: fear, logic, and memory are all vulnerable to decay.
  • Hidden layers: what you see is never the full picture.
  • Encounters are often psychological rather than physical.
  • Environments mutate subtly over time—architecture that shifts, timelines that bend.

📚 Worldbuilding Tips

  • Create a stable surface world—then slowly unravel it.
  • Use dreams, journals, and distorted memories to hint at deeper truths.
  • Write characters that lie or forget things without realizing it.
  • Use ordinary places as anchors: libraries, schools, alleyways—and twist them.

🧹 Narrative Hooks

  • A stranger insists they met you yesterday, but you’ve never seen them before.
  • Your house grows one new room every night. Nobody believes you.
  • The town doesn’t appear on any maps—and you can’t seem to leave.
  • Someone burned every book in the library—but you remember reading them all.

🎮 Game Design Ideas

  • Branching sanity mechanics that alter what the player sees and hears.
  • False clues and red herrings—some placed by entities watching the player.
  • Inventory systems tied to memory: forget an item, and it disappears.
  • Rooms that loop, collapse, or echo decisions made earlier in the game.
The Knower Beneath
A subterranean entity that answers any question—but each answer must be paid with a memory you can never retrieve.
Hollow Prince of the Fourth Bell
An omen of storms and insanity. Always smiling, always behind you in photos. When the bell tolls, he visits.
Celestial Leech
Buried in the sky. Only visible during dreams. It feeds on hope and leaves stains on the stars.
Scriven of the Wrong Page
Appears whenever a book is misprinted. It reads the error aloud, and reality shifts to accommodate the new version.
Elegy of Skin
A song trapped in a vinyl record, said to peel identity from the listener. Often found in sealed basements of forgotten clubs.
Quaint God of Uneven Roads
Blesses lost travelers with visions of the future, but only the worst possible ones. It has hooves and no eyes.
Barrowling Dusk
A fog-creature that mimics loved ones' voices to lure victims deeper into the mist. Always sounds just slightly off.

🗺️ Notable Locations

  • Crucible Hollow – A sinkhole of warped time. Clocks stop here, and compasses spin without rest.
  • Whisper Ridge Sanitorium – Condemned and sealed, yet screams still echo from inside.
  • The Grayed Orchard – Fruit that falls before ripening. Eating it makes your shadow linger behind.
  • The Salt-Hiss Chapel – Church submerged in fog. Choirs sing from beneath the water line.
  • Luminal Station – An abandoned subway station reachable only in dreams.
  • Gorehouse Flats – Empty houses where no lights work—but windows glow when no one is looking.
  • Silent Nest Motel – Staffed entirely by people who share the same face.
Madness doesn't come all at once—it creeps in with familiarity. Build a world that whispers first... and then screams.
