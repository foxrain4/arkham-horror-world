Arkham Horror & Eldritch Madness

Reality crumbles, and you are left to piece together the screaming silence.

Inspired by Arkham Horror and cosmic dread. A world where mystery, madness, and myth intertwine. Clues don’t just reveal answers—they reveal the truth you wish you didn’t know.

💀 What Defines This World Type?

  • Sanity systems: fear, logic, and memory are all vulnerable to decay.
  • Hidden layers: what you see is never the full picture.
  • Encounters are often psychological rather than physical.
  • Environments mutate subtly over time—architecture that shifts, timelines that bend.

📚 Worldbuilding Tips

  • Create a stable surface world—then slowly unravel it.
  • Use dreams, journals, and distorted memories to hint at deeper truths.
  • Write characters that lie or forget things without realizing it.
  • Use ordinary places as anchors: libraries, schools, alleyways—and twist them.

🧹 Narrative Hooks

  • A stranger insists they met you yesterday, but you’ve never seen them before.
  • Your house grows one new room every night. Nobody believes you.
  • The town doesn’t appear on any maps—and you can’t seem to leave.
  • Someone burned every book in the library—but you remember reading them all.

🎮 Game Design Ideas

  • Branching sanity mechanics that alter what the player sees and hears.
  • False clues and red herrings—some placed by entities watching the player.
  • Inventory systems tied to memory: forget an item, and it disappears.
  • Rooms that loop, collapse, or echo decisions made earlier in the game.

🪸 Notable Entities (Pseudo-Monsters)

  • The Eyeless Congregation – A shifting mass of robed figures who chant in reverse. They leave no tracks and cannot be seen in mirrors. They appear only when no one is looking directly at them.
  • Driftmire Womb – A gelatinous crater in the woods that hums with lullabies. If you listen too long, you may remember being born inside it.
  • Archivist of Bone – A librarian entity with too many hands and no face. It collects secrets written in blood and replaces your memories with entries from its own catalog.
  • The Hollow Frequency – A sound that shouldn’t exist, causing radios to bleed and dreams to repeat. Those exposed too long become unable to distinguish which timeline they’re in.
  • Carmine Roots – Vines that pulse like veins and grow only in basements. They whisper suggestions that feel like your own thoughts. One day you might wake up feeding them.
  • Mother of the Ink Sea – A blindfolded being who floats inches above water, trailing quills. It rewrites the past of anyone it touches—but the edits are always cruel.
  • Skelmourne Latch – A locked door that wanders across buildings. If opened, the person disappears, and their reflection starts living their life instead.
  • The Orphaned Choir – Heard but never seen. Their songs unmake nearby objects, erasing names, memories, and meaning itself.
  • Verdance of the Stolen World – A parasitic garden that mimics childhood dreams. Victims wander in, convinced they’re safe, until they take root.
  • The Knower Beneath – A subterranean entity that answers any question—but each answer must be paid with a memory you can never retrieve.
  • Hollow Prince of the Fourth Bell – An omen of storms and insanity. Always smiling, always behind you in photos. When the bell tolls, he visits.
  • Celestial Leech – Buried in the sky. Only visible during dreams. It feeds on hope and leaves stains on the stars.
  • Scriven of the Wrong Page – Appears whenever a book is misprinted. It reads the error aloud, and reality shifts to accommodate the new version.
  • Elegy of Skin – A song trapped in a vinyl record, said to peel identity from the listener. Often found in sealed basements of forgotten clubs.
  • Quaint God of Uneven Roads – Blesses lost travelers with visions of the future, but only the worst possible ones. It has hooves and no eyes.
  • Barrowling Dusk – A fog-creature that mimics loved ones' voices to lure victims deeper into the mist. Always sounds just slightly off.

🗺️ Notable Locations

  • Crucible Hollow – A sinkhole of warped time. Clocks stop here, and compasses spin without rest.
  • Whisper Ridge Sanitorium – Condemned and sealed, yet screams still echo from inside.
  • The Grayed Orchard – Fruit that falls before ripening. Eating it makes your shadow linger behind.
  • The Salt-Hiss Chapel – Church submerged in fog. Choirs sing from beneath the water line.
  • Luminal Station – An abandoned subway station reachable only in dreams.
  • Gorehouse Flats – Empty houses where no lights work—but windows glow when no one is looking.
  • Silent Nest Motel – Staffed entirely by people who share the same face.
Madness doesn't come all at once—it creeps in with familiarity. Build a world that whispers first... and then screams.
